The 2020 National Building Code of Canada is clear about geotechnical accountability, but in Nanaimo, standard grouting templates rarely hold up. The ground here shifts from massive sandstone of the Nanaimo Group to loose glacial till and marine clays within a few metres. A grouting program designed without accounting for this rapid lateral change often misses the target zone entirely. We combine local drilling data with injection parameters tailored to Vancouver Island’s fractured bedrock, where water inflows can exceed 50 L/min in shallow excavations. When foundation rock requires treatment before piling, we cross-reference grouting specs with results from spt-drilling to confirm that the treated mass meets the design bearing capacity.

In Nanaimo, a 3-metre shift in geology can take a grout curtain from effective to pointless. Real-time monitoring during injection is what keeps the program on target.

Technical details of the service in Nanaimo

The work in Nanaimo usually starts with a colloidal mixer paired with a high-shear colloidal mill—this is the only setup that produces a stable suspension in the variable water chemistry of the Nanaimo Group aquifers. We use pneumatic piston pumps rated for continuous injection at 40 bar, which is necessary to penetrate tight sandstone fractures without hydro-jacking the formation. The grout blend is adjusted on site: microfine cements for hairline cracks in sandstone, balanced stable grouts for the coarse colluvium on the slopes near Mount Benson. Real-time pressure, flow, and volume are logged on a data acquisition system that graphs apparent Lugeon values during the pass. This immediate feedback lets the injection supervisor decide whether to thicken the mix or switch to stage grouting without losing the hole. It’s a level of control that makes a difference when the target permeability is below 5 Lugeon for a cutoff curtain.

Risks and considerations in Nanaimo

The most common mistake we see on the island is treating Nanaimo Group sandstone as a homogeneous aquifer. A contractor will set one injection pressure for the entire stage, then watch grout travel 20 metres along an open fracture and surface through a neighbour’s foundation drain. That’s a direct result of ignoring the anisotropy of the rock mass. Another expensive error is using a single thick mix for both the coarse colluvium and the tight sandstone, which plugs the hole early and leaves the finer fractures untreated. A proper grouting design specifies stage length, pressure limits, and refusal criteria that adapt to the ground response. Without that, the owner pays for a curtain that leaks on day one. On projects near the Nanaimo River, the risk compounds because seasonal water level swings can erode ungrouted seams from behind, undermining what looked like a competent cutoff.

Our services

Our grouting design work in Nanaimo covers the full sequence from investigation to verification. Each program is built around the specific permeability target and the geological unit being treated.

Curtain Grouting Design

For cutoff walls below dams and deep excavations. We define hole layout, stage lengths, pressure limits, and refusal criteria based on in-situ packer test data.

Consolidation Grouting

To improve the mass modulus of fractured sandstone under raft foundations or bridge piers. Mix design targets specific Lugeon reduction within the treatment zone.

Compaction Grouting

For loose granular fills and colluvium on Nanaimo’s slopes. We specify low-mobility grout and injection sequence to densify without causing surface heave.

Verification Drilling and Testing

Quick answers

What does grouting design cost for a project in Nanaimo?

For most residential and light commercial projects, a grouting design package falls between CA$1,750 and CA$4,990. The final number depends on the number of injection stages, the target permeability, and how much existing ground data is available.

How do you determine the right injection pressure for Nanaimo Group sandstone?

We use packer tests to measure the hydraulic conductivity of each stage, then set the maximum pressure below the theoretical hydro-jacking limit. During injection, the operator watches for pressure plateaus that signal fracture dilation and adjusts in real time.

Can grouting fix water inflow in a basement excavation near the Nanaimo River?

Yes, it’s a common application here. We design a curtain grouting program around the excavation perimeter, with stage lengths kept short in the upper 3 metres where the overburden is loose. The goal is to reduce inflow to a manageable level for sump pumping.

What’s the difference between permeation and compaction grouting?

Permeation grouting fills the pores and fractures in rock or sand without changing the soil structure, using low-viscosity grout. Compaction grouting injects a stiff, low-mobility grout that physically displaces and densifies the surrounding soil—it’s better for loose fill and colluvium.

How long does it take to prepare a grouting design?

A straightforward program for a single-family foundation can be ready in a week once the site investigation data is in. For a municipal cutoff curtain or dam rehabilitation, expect two to three weeks to complete the analysis and detailed injection plan.
